Common Questions
Frequently Asked
Is it free to list my trade business?›
Yes — the Starter tier is completely free. You get a full public profile, searchable listing, and up to 10 booking requests per month. Paid tiers unlock unlimited bookings, featured placement, and advanced analytics.
How are contractors verified?›
Contractors self-declare their trade license numbers (ESA, TSSA, TSSA Gas, CWB, etc.) upon registration. Our team cross-references submitted license numbers against Ontario regulatory databases. Verified badges are issued to providers who pass this check.
Can I book a contractor for emergency service?›
Yes. Many contractors on BookYourTrades offer emergency service. When browsing the directory, filter by trade type and city, then review individual provider profiles — emergency availability and response times are listed on each profile.
What types of projects is BookYourTrades suited for?›
BookYourTrades is built for commercial, industrial, and institutional (ICI) projects. This includes office buildings, retail, warehouses, manufacturing facilities, healthcare, hospitality, and municipal infrastructure. Residential projects are not the primary focus of this platform.
How does the booking system work for contractors?›
When a client submits a booking request through your profile, it appears in your dashboard under "Pending." You review the job details, then Confirm or Decline. Confirmed appointments appear in your schedule. You can also mark jobs as Completed once done.
Can I list multiple locations or trade types?›
The Professional tier supports a single listing with multiple service areas. The Enterprise tier supports multiple distinct business locations or trade types under one account. Contact us to set up an Enterprise account.

Career Pathways

How to Get Into the Trades in Ontario

A complete guide for high school students and career-changers. Everything you need to know about apprenticeships, schooling, Red Seal certification, and launching a trades career in Ontario.

Why Skilled Trades?

The Best-Kept Career Secret in Canada

Trades careers in Ontario offer exceptional wages, job security, and the ability to run your own business — without a four-year degree.

💰
$80K–$120K+ Salaries
Journeyman electricians, plumbers, and pipefitters routinely earn $85–$120K+ per year in Ontario. Master tradespeople running their own shops earn significantly more.
🏗️
Earn While You Learn
Unlike university, apprentices earn a wage from day one — starting around $18–$25/hr and increasing each year. No six-figure student debt. You graduate earning full journeyman wages.
📈
Critical Shortage
Ontario is projected to need 100,000+ new tradespeople by 2030. Skilled tradespeople have some of the lowest unemployment rates of any profession in Canada.
The Pathway

Step-by-Step: From High School to Journeyman

1
Explore in High School
Ontario's Specialist High Skills Major (SHSM) program lets students earn trade-related certifications and co-op hours in Grade 11–12. Many school boards also offer dual credit programs at local colleges. The Ontario Youth Apprenticeship Program (OYAP) lets Grade 11–12 students register as apprentices and start accumulating hours while still in school.
oyap.com ↗  |  ontario.ca/apprenticeship ↗
2
Find an Employer / Sponsor
You must be employed by a licensed contractor in your trade before you can register as an apprentice. This is called finding a "sponsoring employer." Browse the BookYourTrades Jobs Board for companies actively hiring apprentices. Union halls (IBEW, UA, SMWIA) also recruit and place apprentices — contact your local directly.
3
Register Your Apprenticeship
Once you have a sponsoring employer, both you and your employer register with the Ontario College of Trades (OCOT) through the Skills Development Fund. You'll receive a Training Agreement that outlines the hours, in-school requirements, and wages for each year of your apprenticeship. Registration is free for the apprentice.
Register at ontario.ca ↗
4
Complete In-School Training
Apprenticeships combine on-the-job hours with mandatory in-school blocks at a college or apprenticeship training centre. For example, an electrician apprenticeship (309A) requires 9,000 hours over ~5 years with annual 8-week in-school blocks. In-school training is covered by Employment Insurance (EI) — apply through Service Canada before your school block starts.
5
Write Your Certificate of Qualification (C of Q)
After completing your hours and in-school requirements, you write the Certificate of Qualification exam through the Ontario College of Trades. Passing this exam makes you a Journeyman in your trade. For compulsory trades (like electrician or plumber), this is legally required before you can work independently.
6
Red Seal (Optional) & Running Your Own Business
The Red Seal (Interprovincial Standards) exam is optional but allows you to work in any province without re-certifying. After gaining journeyman experience, many tradespeople pursue a Master certification (required to pull permits in many trades) and eventually start their own company.
